# CS2 Market Analyzer — Product Brief

## Brand & Purpose

**What is this?** A minimal, functional market analytics tool for CS2 players. Real-time and historical price tracking without noise—think Bloomberg terminal for weapon skins.

**Users:** Primarily CS2 traders and collectors optimizing their portfolios. Secondary users: market analysts studying price trends.

**Core promise:** See what's moving, how fast, and what it cost yesterday. Fast. No fluff.

## Tone & Voice

- **Precise over persuasive.** Data first, opinions only when data backs them.
- **Functional, not friendly.** This is a work tool. Efficiency > warmth.
- **Jargon OK.** Users know CS2 terms; don't dumb it down.
- **No hype.** No "revolutionary," no marketing copy. Just the facts.

## Anti-references

- **Not Robinhood or crypto dashboards** (trendy, marketing-heavy, flashy).
- **Not minimal design blogs** (beautiful but precious—every element justified, not decorative).
- **Not Reddit-adjacent** (loud, meme-heavy, community-focused).

Reference instead: **Bloomberg Terminal, TradingView light theme, stock tickers.** Serious, dense, fast.

## Strategic Principles

1. **Data density over simplicity.** Fit more meaningful data per screen, not less. No whitespace waste.
2. **Real-time first.** If something can update live, it should. Lag is trust-breaking.
3. **Discoverable, not guided.** Let users find what they need. No tutorials, no onboarding flows.
4. **Keyboard power users.** Shortcuts matter. Copy prices, switch tabs, navigate fast.

## Register

**product** — design serves the data and workflows, not the reverse.

## Key Features (Phase 1)

1. **Market overview:** All items, current price, 24h change, volume
2. **Historical trends:** Price charts (7d, 30d, all-time), trendlines
3. **Item detail:** Price history, volume, rarity/category info, alerts (if time)
4. **Search & filter:** By name, category, price range, volatility

## Primary Use Cases

1. **Quick price check:** "What's the AWP Dragon Lore price right now?"
2. **Trend hunting:** "Which items have spiked in the last week?"
3. **Portfolio tracking:** "How much are my items worth now vs. when I bought them?"
